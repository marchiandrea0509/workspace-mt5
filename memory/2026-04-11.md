# 2026-04-11

- In Discord `#mt5`, investigated a laptop hard shutdown that interrupted the MT5/OpenClaw session.
- Default MT5 automation target remained the portable instance (`C:\MT5_OANDA_PAPER_OC\MQL5` via `mql5/MQL5`), but immediately after the shutdown that portable terminal was not running, while the old AppData-backed OANDA terminal had auto-restarted and re-synced to the broker.
- Account/broker state survived the shutdown cleanly: OANDA paper account re-authorized successfully and still showed `0 positions` / `28 orders`.
- `scripts\reload_mt5_bridge.ps1 -InstanceName oanda_paper_oc` successfully restarted the portable MT5 terminal, but the restart verification failed because `GrayPaperBridgeEA` did not auto-load on a chart after restart. This confirmed a specific shutdown weak point: the portable terminal can come back logged in without the bridge EA attached/initialized.
- Recovery required a manual reattach of `GrayPaperBridgeEA` in the portable MT5 window plus Algo Trading enabled. After that, health check confirmed both the EA load line and the init line (`Watching gray_bridge\inbox`).
- Cleanup after recovery: closed the old AppData-backed fallback MT5 process and also closed an older duplicate portable MT5 process, leaving one healthy portable MT5 process running as the sole bridge target.
- Added `docs/MT5_HARD_SHUTDOWN_RECOVERY_PLAN.md` with the observed failure mode, the operator recovery sequence, and a staged plan for future automated recovery tasks after hard shutdowns.
